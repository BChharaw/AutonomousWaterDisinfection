What is UVCC?

The UVCC (Ultraviolet Conduit Cell) is a prototype built by the 2022 Clarkson SS Engineering Idol design team. It is a proposed part of our concept solution to address the ongoing boil water advisory in the North Caribou Lake first nations community, which has an undetermined lift date. As the water in the community is contaminated with E. coli, our system is primarily designed to kill the E. coli present in their water. Our system was designed to be ultra-compact (to aid in transportation), durable, and most importantly cost-effective. With a budget of under $150, we designed a system capable of sterilizing (in 24h) over 23 times what the average household uses per day! (based on E. coli the target bacteria of our prototype, the bacteria present in the water at the North Caribou Lake first nations community.)

What can the UVCC kill

The UVCC can kill any organism suspended in water including bacteria, viruses, protozoa, spores, algae, and other microorganisms. This program is mean to illustrate the various flow rates that our system will need to run at, depending on the organism it is intending to kill. Our system is specifically designed to have an adjustable flow rate, something which could be automated in future versions of our prototype.
