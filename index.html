THE UVCC

Autonomous Water Sterilization - JavaScript, C++, HTML/CSS, and Bootstrap

What is The UVCC?

The UVCC (Ultraviolet Conduit Cell) is a water sterilization prototype built by the 2022 CLK Engineering Idol design team, designed to autonomously disinfect pathogen-contaminated water.

Designed to be compact (to aid in transportation to communities under boil-water advisories), cost-effective, and highly scalable, the prototype we manufactured is capable of sterilizing over 36 000 plastic water bottles worth of water in a day.

Moreover, this is the equivalent of how much 29 Canadian households use in a day! These numbers are based on average household usage of 630L of water per day and a standard 500ml water bottle with E. coli disinfection of 99.99%.

My role in the project

Within this team, I designed a fluid analysis algorithm in JavaScript using object-oriented programming which computes the physics requisite to determine what flowrate our system is safe to run at. This algorithm optimized the flow rate of the prototype by 30% by decreasing the minimum tollerance for error with the flow rate. This fluid analysis was heavily used to implement design decisions as we could responsively see how our system would react. What can the UVCC kill

The UVCC can kill any organism suspended in water including bacteria, viruses, protozoa, spores, algae, and other microorganisms. This is as the ultraviolet wavelength emited by the bulb destroys any RNA or DNA in the organism preventing them from reproducing. Depending on the organism that is being targeted it will run at different flow rates to ensure the safety of the water. How it works

The mechanics of the prototype are fundamentally based on water flowing past a sterilization bulb which emits light at a wavelength optimized to disinfect. The water is pumped through using a pump with a variable flow rate which is controled by a waterproof servo and measured using a hall-effect flow rate sensor. This allows the water to dynamically controlled at a rate which is deemed safe based on the software fluid analysis algorithm, allowing the system to be effective with changing water pressure. Moreover, there is a photodiode and a small LED at opposite points of the circumference and this setup allows the system to calibrates the relative turbidity of the water and adjust the flow rate to ensure that the UVC sterilization bulb is effective. Finally the system is lined which a reflective sheathing to ensure maximum light exposure, and a buzzer alarm and killswitch allowing the system to disconnect from power and shut off the water supply if a leak is detected or the water is moving at an unsafe rate.
